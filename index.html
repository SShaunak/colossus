ABOUT US

What is Colossus?

Colossus is one of Don Bosco College's more well-known events. Organized by the Department of Computer Applications and named after the world's earliest computers, this tech fest is a celebration of the vast field of computer sciences. BCA and BSc students from across the state have been coming, year after year, to participate in events that test your intellect and knowledge, as much as they test your creativity and out-of-the-box thinking.


After sucessfully creating an equally challenging and fun experience for participants for the past eight years, Colossus is back, for the 9th time! With past experience under our belt and an organising committee that comprises of the fresh, young minds of our students - Colossus 9.0 is set to be the biggest one yet! If you haven't already, register your team now for Colossus 9.0 from the 28th and 29th of February, 2020.

DON BOSCO'S BCA PROGRAM



The BCA Course was the first course to be started at Don Bosco College, in 2001. The department has 4 computer laboratories, with the latest technology computers, laptops and other peripherals. The department is enrolled as a member of the Computer Society of India


Don Bosco's BCA Program also regularly includes courses and workshops focused on building modern, relevent skills - like Iot, App Development, Arduino, etc. This focus on all-round learning has made the BCA department an incubator for young minds to thrive and grow!
